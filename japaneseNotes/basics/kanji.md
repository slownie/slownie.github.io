# What is Kanji?
Arguably the hardest part of learning Japanese, Kanji are Chinese characters that have been adapted into the Japanese language. 

This means that every character uses the phonetic sounds found in Hiragana and Katakana.

Kanji are ideograms, characters that have their own meaning. They can also be combined together to create a new word.

For example,  雨 (rain), 田 (rice paddy), 乚 (umbrella) create 電, which means electricity. 

These components of kanji are called 'Radicals' and will be discussed in the next section.

# How many Kanji are there?
Most estimate that there are between 50,000 and 85,000 kanji. This is because there is no definite count of all kanji in existence. 

Thankfully, you will not need to know every single kanji character to be fluent in Japanese.

Only around 2,136 of these kanji are used in daily conversations and are referred to as 'Jōyō Kanji'.

These characters are categorized into elementary school grades ranging from 1 to 6, while also having a secondary school category.

# Onyomi and Kunyomi
Due to how kanji were adapted into Japanese, most kanji have two readings referred to as Onyomi and Kunyomi.

Onyomi is the original Chinese pronunciation of the character.

Kunyomi is the Japanese pronunciation of the character.

You must know both to be fluent in Japanese as there are instances where you will have to use one or the other, they are NOT interchangeable.
