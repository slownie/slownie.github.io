# What is a Particle?
Particles are used to indicate the grammatical function of the word it is attached to.

They are written in Hiragana and are easy to identify when used alongside Kanji.

Particles are incredibly important to understand as they can change the entire meaning of a sentence when used.

Not only that, but particles determine what does what in a sentence, not the order the words in a sentence. 


## は - Topic Indicator
は (pronounced 'wa') indicates the topic of the sentence.

**Example:**  
あさんはべんごしです。  
"Mr. A is a lawyer."

In this sentence, Mr. A is the topic under discussion and lawyer is the noun used to describe him.

In Japanese, the topic of a sentence is very similar to the subject in other languages.

But, they are different as it also possible for an object to be the topic in Japanese.
