## です
Desu (pronounced des), is used to grammatically link subjects and predicates.  
Its the equivalent to "to be" or "it is" in English.

## だ
Da is the more informal version of Desu, and works in the same ways.  
It is commonly when with people you're familiar with like friends for family.  

## でございます
Degozaimasu is the more formal version of Desu, and works in the same ways.  
It is commonly used in very formal situations like at interviews, if you're serving someone, e.t.c.

## ます
Masu is the formal ending of a verb.

**Example:**  
りんごをたべます。  
"I eat apples".

## か
Ka indicates that the sentence is a question.   
It is the equivalent of a question mark in Japanese.

**Example:**  
だれですか。
"Who is it?"

## ね
Ne is used to seek confirmation, but it is not asking a question like か is.  
If spoken with a rising intonation, then the speaker wants to receive confirmation.  
If spoken with a falling intonation, then the speaker is agreeing.  

**Example:**  
さむいですね。 
"It is cold isn't it?"

## よ
Yo is used when the speaker is sure of what they're saying.  
It is the equivalent of an exclamation mark in Japanese.

**Example:**  
このほんはつまらないですよ。  
"This book is boring."
